Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are a classic and beloved treat that features a buttery, golden-brown cookie dough studded with morsels of rich, semi-sweet chocolate. These cookies have a slightly crisp exterior and a soft, chewy interior, making them irresistible. When freshly baked, the chocolate chips are melty and gooey, adding to the indulgent experience.

Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 10 minutes
  • Preparation: 5 minutes
  • Cooking: 5 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large eggs
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of butter or oil
  • Optional fillings: cheese, diced vegetables, cooked meats, herbs

Instructions

  1. Start by poaching the eggs: Fill a saucepan with water and vinegar, bring to a gentle simmer, and carefully crack the eggs into the simmering water. Cook for 4-5 minutes until the whites are set, and the yolks are still runny.
  2. While the eggs are poaching, toast the English muffins and cook the Canadian bacon or ham slices until lightly browned.
  3. Make the hollandaise sauce: In a small saucepan, whisk together egg yolks, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Cook over low heat, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens slightly.
  4. Gradually whisk in melted butter until the sauce is smooth and thickened.
  5. Assemble the dish: Place a toasted English muffin half on a plate, top with a slice of Canadian bacon or ham, then carefully place a poached egg on top.
  6. Drizzle the hollandaise sauce over the egg, garnish with chopped parsley or chives, and serve immediately.
